# The Last Page of the Phone Book

At the back of the last phone book for our town were the emergency numbers, the public works listings, and a silence larger than paper.

I kept it for years because it represented a world in which everyone could, at least in principle, be found. That world has died. We now locate one another through platforms, algorithms, hints, and a capacity for low-level surveillance once reserved for states.

Still, sometimes I open the book just to remember the democratic plainness of it: names in one typeface, each life granted identical confidence in print.
