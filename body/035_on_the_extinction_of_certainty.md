# On the Extinction of Certainty

Certainty used to roam in great herds.

You can see this in old speeches, inherited rules, and the way earlier generations built furniture as if the future had signed a contract. Certainty occupied parlors, pulpits, and railway timetables. It bred well in empire and badly in kitchens.

Then evidence, migration, weather, memory, and ordinary disappointment reduced its habitat. It did not vanish overnight. It merely lost the right to call itself nature.

Some mourn this. I don't. Uncertainty, though messier, is more hospitable to kindness. It allows revision. It leaves room at the table for strangers and facts.

If certainty is dead, let us thank the scavengers.
