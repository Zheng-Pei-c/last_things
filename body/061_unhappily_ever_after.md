# Unhappily Ever After

The princess married, the dragon was audited, and the kingdom entered a long period of administrative competence.

This did not satisfy anyone.

The story had promised a permanent arrangement of joy, but joy turned out to require maintenance, and maintenance is bad for legend. The couple argued over curtains. The former dragon missed purpose. Farmers petitioned against symbolic weather. Children asked whether happiness had to look so much like paperwork.

And yet, beneath the disappointment, something sturdier grew. Not bliss. Not glory. A life with compost, taxes, illness, jokes, and the occasional astonishing pear.

In later centuries this was regarded as a better ending, though a poorer slogan.
