# The Day the Streetlamps Went Extinct

The city replaced the old streetlamps with efficient white strips fixed to the undersides of concrete ambition.

No one held a funeral for the lamps with their amber halos and dependable melancholy. Yet the night changed character immediately. It lost its talent for confession. Lovers no longer paused under pools of forgiving light. Cats became municipal instead of mythical.

At first people praised the brightness. Then they noticed they were lonelier in it.

So a painter, an electrician, and one retired thief began rescuing broken lamp heads from scrapyards. They wired them secretly along the river path. The city called it unauthorized infrastructure. Everyone else called it evening.
