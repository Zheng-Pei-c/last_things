# The Library of Unfinished Goodbyes

There is, under the old post office and two flights below municipal memory, a library of unfinished goodbyes.

On its shelves are farewell letters never mailed, phone calls cut by pride, last conversations interrupted by taxis, visas, storms, stubbornness, and death's appalling sense of timing. The librarians wear soft shoes and do not ask what brought anyone there.

Visitors may request their own shelf if they know the date approximately.

A woman came once and found, between a missed apology and a postponed confession, the goodbye she had never said to the city where she had been twenty-two. It was shorter than expected. Most true goodbyes are. She copied it onto the back of a train ticket and left feeling less haunted, though no more complete.
