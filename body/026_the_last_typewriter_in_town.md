# The Last Typewriter in Town

The last typewriter in town lived in a watch repair shop among miniature screwdrivers and clocks too ashamed to admit defeat.

People came to see it as if it were a whale skeleton or a saint. Mostly they wanted to touch the keys and hear certainty happen one letter at a time.

When it finally broke, the repairman closed the shop for two days. On the third day he placed the machine in the window with a card that read:

> DEAD, BUT STILL CORRECT ABOUT MANY THINGS.

A week later a group of students started meeting there with notebooks. They wrote badly and with conviction. The typewriter, though still dead, appeared satisfied.
