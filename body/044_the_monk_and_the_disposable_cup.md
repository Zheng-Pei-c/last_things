# The Monk and the Disposable Cup

A monk found a disposable cup lodged in reeds beside the monastery pond and gave it a funeral.

The novices objected. "It is plastic," they said.

"Exactly," he replied. "It has outlived its own intention and now suffers from purposeless immortality. We must assist."

They buried the cup in a shoebox with a prayer for all objects designed to be used once and remembered for centuries.

Years later one novice, now an engineer, credited that funeral with his entire career in repair.
