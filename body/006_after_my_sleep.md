# After My Sleep

She went to sleep convinced she would wake as herself.

That confidence struck her now as youthful.

In the night, several things expired: yesterday's grievance, an ambition involving a blue coat, the last reliable version of her mother's face, and whatever arrangement had allowed her to believe that continuity was natural.

When she woke, she recognized the furniture but not the owner. The room was hers in the way a river is someone's if they have crossed it often. The body knew the route to coffee. The mouth knew her name. Yet something had ended during sleep and been replaced by a quieter tenant.

At first she mourned. By noon she was reorganizing the books.

Toward evening she understood that this happens all the time. People call it rest because the truth would frighten the neighbors.
