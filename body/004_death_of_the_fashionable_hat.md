# The Death of the Fashionable Hat

There was a year in which every intelligent person wore the same ridiculous hat.

It was too tall for sincerity and too soft for dignity. It bent in rain. It entered rooms before its owner and left after the conversation had collapsed. Poets wore it because they feared being mistaken for accountants. Accountants wore it because they feared being mistaken for poets.

Then a photograph surfaced of nineteen ministers standing in a row in the identical hat, all of them looking like underfed mushrooms. Public affection snapped at once. By Tuesday the hat was irony. By Thursday it was apology. By the following week it existed only on heads too stubborn to evolve.

Years later, children discovered one in a trunk and asked, "What was this for?"

"Status," said the grandmother.

"Did it work?"

She looked at the hat, and because age is a great editor, she answered correctly.

"Only until the weather changed."
