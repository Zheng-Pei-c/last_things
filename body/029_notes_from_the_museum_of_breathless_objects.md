# Notes from the Museum of Breathless Objects

The museum had no famous collection, only a patient one.

Room 1 held things that had ceased but not vanished: a cassette ribbon in a glass dish, a theater ticket from a language reform conference, the key to a bakery demolished for progress, one cracked bowl from a marriage that did not fail so much as conclude.

Room 2 was for unofficial extinctions. Here lay the last use of "therefore" in ordinary conversation, the final neighborhood where children played unsupervised until the streetlights came on, and a taxidermied confidence in endless growth.

Visitors often left smiling, which troubled the board.

The curator explained this was common. "People come expecting doom," she said, "and discover compost."
