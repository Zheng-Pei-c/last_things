# The Comet in Divorce Proceedings

The comet claimed it had been misrepresented.

For centuries painters had depicted it as omen, sword, celestial threat. Kings blamed it for plagues. Priests borrowed its tail for sermons. Lovers pointed and made promises the comet had never authorized.

Now, in the formal language of the court, it sought separation from symbolism.

"I am ice, debris, and obedient mathematics," it testified. "I am not your war, your prophecy, or your interesting baby."

The judge, a moon of considerable age, granted the petition partially. The comet was freed from harvest omens, dynastic collapse, and minor skin conditions, but remained legally responsible for wonder.

It left the courtroom brighter than when it arrived.
