# The One Thousand and First Death

After the thousand and first death, the book cleared its throat.

Pages rustled like leaves discussing weather. The stories, having buried men, stars, habits, customs, words, arguments, fashions, cities, maps, empires, accents, marriages, gods, and naps, found themselves confronted by a more difficult corpse: the idea that ending was singular.

This final death belonged to that idea.

Once it was gone, everything grew embarrassingly alive. Each grave developed a door. Each farewell acquired a corridor. The readers closed the book and discovered, with mild irritation and some delight, that they had been handed not a mausoleum but a seed catalog.

Outside, someone yawned awake from an afternoon sleep and entered the room as if invented there.
