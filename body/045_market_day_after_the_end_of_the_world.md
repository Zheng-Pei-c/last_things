# Market Day After the End of the World

The end of the world was on a Thursday, which annoyed the vegetable sellers.

There had been sirens, announcements, a brief celestial dispute, and one appalling orange light in the east. By evening everything important had concluded. Empires, debt structures, cable news, the accepted distinction between premium and standard tomatoes.

On Friday the market reopened.

A woman sold carrots from a crate stamped with the logo of a civilization no longer operational. A boy held up fish and shouted prices into the cleanest silence anyone could remember. Two former consultants argued over the correct exchange rate between onions and batteries.

"Shouldn't we be grieving?" asked a traveler, arriving late to apocalypse as to everything else.

The carrot seller shrugged. "This is grieving. Also breakfast."

By noon someone was flirting near the herb stall, which proved the species recoverable.
