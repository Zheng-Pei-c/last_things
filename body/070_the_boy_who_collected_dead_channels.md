# The Boy Who Collected Dead Channels

In the age after cable but before common sense, a boy named Ren learned to love television channels that no longer existed.

He found them in forum archives, in old remotes, in the speech of adults who still said things like "back when channel 48 showed weather all day." To him these vanished stations formed a private mythology: the station that sold knives at 3 a.m., the one that played black-and-white films nobody admitted watching, the local public channel where a woman taught calligraphy to twelve loyal insomniacs.

He kept a notebook of their deaths. Cause of death: merger. Cause of death: deregulation. Cause of death: attention.

When he grew up he made a website where each dead channel reappeared as a small room. People entered, listened, remembered. The channels were still gone, of course. But gone had become visitable.
