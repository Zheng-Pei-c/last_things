# Dialogue with a Dead Metaphor

"You can't keep calling heartbreak a shipwreck," said the editor.

"Why not?"

"Because everyone has already drowned in it."

The metaphor, hearing this, coughed seawater onto the carpet and expired.

We buried it beside a dead phoenix and a horse no one had ridden since the nineteenth century.

For several weeks I wrote literal sentences and was miserable. Then one morning I compared grief to a supermarket at closing time and felt the language shiver back to life.
