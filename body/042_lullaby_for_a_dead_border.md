# Lullaby for a Dead Border
::: poem
Sleep now, line on the map.  
No guard is feeding you.  
The river has already  
licked your ink apart.

Sleep, old checkpoint booth.  
Your stamps are full of silence.  
Children are crossing you  
on bicycles of grammar.

Sleep, and do not worry.  
The languages you separated  
have married in the market.  
Their children are beautiful and difficult.
:::
