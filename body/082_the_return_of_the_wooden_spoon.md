# The Return of the Wooden Spoon

My mother threw away the old wooden spoon after the handle split.

A month later my brother brought home a new one from a market across town. It had the same scar near the bowl, the same dark grain like weathered honey, the same improbable authority over soups. My mother laughed so hard she had to sit down.

"They never leave," she said, meaning spoons, mothers, habits, maybe all useful things.
