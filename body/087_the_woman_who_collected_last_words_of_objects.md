# The Woman Who Collected Last Words of Objects

Mrs. Ahn claimed that objects had last words if you listened correctly.

The kettle's were always impatient. The umbrella usually died saying, "I warned you." Shoes tended toward silence, except formal shoes, which expired in a cloud of self-importance. Refrigerators gave long electrical farewells no one deserved.

When her own radio finally failed after forty years, she held it to her ear and smiled. "Ah," she said, "it remembers a song before I was born."

She wrote that down and put the radio on the shelf.
