# When the Map Died

The map died when the road moved.

For years it had hung in the bus station, preserving a town that no longer existed in such tidy proportions. The bakery had become a pharmacy, the river had become a complaint, and the district called New Extension had grown old enough to resent the title. Yet the map persisted, confidently wrong.

A little girl stared at it one afternoon and asked her father, "Where is the place with all this certainty?"

That question finished it.

The station master took the map down. On the blank wall behind it remained a cleaner rectangle, as if truth itself had been sun-faded around the edges.
