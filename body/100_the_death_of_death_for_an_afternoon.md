# The Death of Death for an Afternoon

Once, by clerical error or cosmic mercy, death itself took an afternoon off.

Doctors were bewildered. Executioners became philosophers. Flowers refused to wilt on schedule. A fly struck a window twelve times and emerged with improved self-esteem. Across the world, people who had expected catastrophe experienced instead a profound logistical delay.

Nothing could conclude. Arguments remained open like government cases. A theater production ran into the street because no scene could close. Even the sea looked indecisive at the shore.

By dusk everyone understood the problem. Without ending, beginning also stalled. Babies took longer to arrive. Bread would not brown. Kisses lingered past romance into administration.

When death returned, apologetic and carrying paperwork, the world received it with surprising gratitude.
