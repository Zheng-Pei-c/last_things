# The Day My Accent Died

Her accent did not die heroically. It faded under fluorescent office lights.

Each meeting sanded it flatter. Each polite repetition of her own name replaced one familiar angle with a safer sound. She told herself this was adaptation, and perhaps it was. But on the train home one evening she called her sister and heard, in the first impatient word out of her mouth, the old accent rush back like weather through an open door.

That was when she understood the death had been exaggerated. The accent was not gone. It had gone into hiding until tenderness required it.
