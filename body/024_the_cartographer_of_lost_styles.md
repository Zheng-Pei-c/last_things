# The Cartographer of Lost Styles

Madame Verglas kept a private atlas of vanished ways of being elegant.

Here was the chapter on people who once crossed rooms as if entering treaties. Here the district where sarcasm wore gloves. Here the extinct peninsula of handwriting that leaned forward with optimism. Here the islands of theatrical sincerity, now mostly underwater.

She updated the atlas obsessively, drawing little skulls where a gesture had gone missing. But age softened her scholarship. In the margins she began adding arrows: *reappeared in teenagers*, *survives in bus drivers*, *possibly nesting in drag shows*.

At ninety, she admitted the map had been wrong all along. Styles did not die. They moulted, hid, and returned wearing cheaper shoes.
