# Letter Found in the Mouth of a Fish

Dear Former Beloved,

By the time this reaches you, I will no longer hold the opinion that ruined us.

For years I cherished it like a private jewel: that love should remain unchanged or else admit defeat. It seemed noble at twenty-one and absurd by thirty. Yet I defended it stubbornly, and so the thing itself had to die in order for me to continue with any dignity.

Please understand: I do not write to revive our old arrangement. I write only to report a death and its curious sequel. Since that opinion expired, I have become fond of revisions. I move the furniture. I eat tomatoes in winter. I forgive with a delay that is nearly elegant.

If you still hate me, hate the previous edition.

With revised regards,  
L.
