# Manual for Retiring an Ancestor

Some ancestors work too hard.

They linger in family stories long after their lessons have spoiled. They govern marriages from framed photographs. They sit in kitchens made entirely of expectation.

When this occurs, proceed gently.

1. Thank the ancestor for surviving enough to become inconvenient.
2. Wash the inherited shame in warm water.
3. Keep the recipe. Discard the prohibition.
4. Say aloud: *Your fear is finished here.*
5. Open a window.

If the ancestor refuses retirement, give them a small garden in memory and decline further instructions.
