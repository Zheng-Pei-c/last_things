# The Funeral of Bad Taste

Bad taste died flamboyantly, which was in character.

The coffin was lacquered chartreuse. The flowers argued with one another. Half the mourners arrived in sequins at noon. The other half wore black but accessorized with feathers the size of domestic birds.

The priest, who secretly admired the deceased, declared that bad taste had been a pioneer of sincerity. "It loved too much," he said. "Color, leopard print, sorrow, gold trim, novelty fountains. It lacked restraint, yes. But unlike refinement, it was never cruel on purpose."

At the graveside, refined taste fainted from exposure.

By evening everyone was dancing on the mound in shoes no magazine would endorse. Several excellent outfits were born on the spot.
