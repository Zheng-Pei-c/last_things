# The Bee and the Extinct Garden

A bee returned to the lot where a grandmother's garden had been and found only parking lines.

It circled above the asphalt in visible disbelief. For one ridiculous second, a woman standing nearby wanted to apologize on behalf of zoning, inheritance, and modern taste.

Then, from a crack near space B14, a volunteer marigold lifted its orange head like a joke with municipal consequences. The bee landed. History revised itself by one small buzzing paragraph.
