# The Piano in the Flooded School

After the flood, the school gym smelled of mud, bleach, and arithmetic interrupted.

In the music room the upright piano had taken in water up to middle C. The teachers called it ruined. The janitor, who distrusted final judgments, let it dry for months beside open windows. Its wood warped; two keys developed personalities; one entire register sounded like memory trying to pass as weather.

At the reopening ceremony a child played it badly and beautifully. No one asked for the old sound back. They had all changed too much to deserve it.
