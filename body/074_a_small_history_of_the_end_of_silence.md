# A Small History of the End of Silence

Silence used to possess acreage.

It lived between villages, in libraries before espresso machines, in letters crossing the sea, in evenings before everyone carried a lit opinion in a pocket. Then machinery, media, commerce, and loneliness subdivided the land.

Silence survives now in fragments: the held breath before bad news, snowfall at four a.m., the second after a pianist lifts both hands. It is endangered, yes, but still breeding in remote valleys of attention.
