# Poem for a Suit Left in a Closet
::: poem
The suit remembers shoulders  
that no longer report for duty.  
It keeps the dark shape of ambition  
in breathable wool.

Buttons hold their tiny planets.  
The lining still whispers hurry.  
Yet dust has begun  
its silver revisions.

One day a nephew will try it on,  
laugh at the cut, keep the pockets,  
and walk out wearing  
a future altered at the hem.
:::
