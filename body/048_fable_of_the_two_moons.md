# Fable of the Two Moons

Once there were two moons over a country too ambitious for astronomy.

The first moon was old, pockmarked, patient, and reliable as unpaid labor. The second was new, launched by the government to symbolize progress, innovation, and several tax exemptions. It shone in patriotic colors and delivered weather, surveillance, and targeted advertisements for national resilience.

People admired the second moon for three months.

Then it began interrupting sleep with updates.

The old moon, which had survived wolves, tides, poems, and empires, said nothing. It merely continued appearing at the correct angle for longing. Eventually the new moon burned out from relevance. It fell into the sea with a noise like a campaign promise meeting geology.

The fishermen, who had trusted the old moon all along, kept working by its light.
