# The Last Policeman of the Old Morality

He patrolled the neighborhood in a hat already ridiculous in his own decade, issuing judgments for skirts, laughter, late dinners, and women who smoked as if they owned both lungs and evening.

For a time he was feared. Fear, however, has poor maintenance. One by one the alleged offenders aged into authority. The skirts entered museums. The laughter inherited property. The women who smoked became grandmothers whose recipes nobody dared challenge.

By the end he was merely a man with a whistle full of dead weather.
