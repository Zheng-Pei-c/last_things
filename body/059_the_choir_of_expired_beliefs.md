# The Choir of Expired Beliefs

In the basement of the old civic hall rehearsed a choir composed entirely of expired beliefs.

There sang the idea that hard work guarantees reward, the notion that empires civilize, the suspicion that men are improved by silence, the confidence that progress is linear, and one stubborn theory of diet from 1986. Their harmonies were uneven but educational.

The conductor, a former teacher with excellent wrists, did not try to revive them. He trained them only to hold pitch long enough for the audience to hear what had once sounded convincing.

At the end of each performance, the choir dissolved into discussion. This was considered the encore.
