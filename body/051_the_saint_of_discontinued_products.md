# The Saint of Discontinued Products

In a side chapel behind the train station there stood, unofficially, the saint of discontinued products.

No doctrine supported this. The candle stand was improvised from an old display rack. Yet pilgrims came steadily: women carrying cracked rice cookers no company would repair, men with obsolete chargers, teenagers mourning phones too unfashionable to update, one librarian with a machine that only printed labels if addressed affectionately.

They prayed not for miracles exactly, but for continuity. Sometimes they received it. A retired engineer might emerge from the crowd, squint at the appliance, and say, "I think the problem is grief disguised as wiring."

In this way dead products continued briefly, and the city acquired a modest theology of maintenance.
