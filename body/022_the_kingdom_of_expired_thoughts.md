# The Kingdom of Expired Thoughts

There is, somewhere behind the forehead and slightly to the left of regret, a kingdom where dead thoughts go when they can no longer justify the rent.

There reign abandoned certainties in threadbare robes. There stale opinions wander the plazas asking if anyone still finds them useful. There one can hear the clatter of obsolete moral machinery and the sobbing of predictions made with confidence in 2007.

One feverish traveler arrived there once.

The border guards examined the traveler's pockets and confiscated three inherited prejudices, two literary poses, and a political despair carried for decorative purposes. They stamped the traveler's hand with invisible ink and let the traveler pass.

At the center of the kingdom stood a market selling compost. "For what?" the traveler asked.

"New ideas," the vendor replied. "What else did you think the old ones were for?"
