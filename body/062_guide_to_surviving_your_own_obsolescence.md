# Guide to Surviving Your Own Obsolescence

When informed that you have become outdated, remain calm.

Do not attempt immediate reinvention in front of witnesses.

First determine whether you are truly obsolete or merely unfashionable. The difference is crucial and often profitable.

Second, inspect younger versions of yourself for theft. Many so-called innovations are old habits with brighter packaging.

Third, keep one useful function nobody has bothered to improve.

Finally, accept that obsolescence is often a corridor, not a grave.
