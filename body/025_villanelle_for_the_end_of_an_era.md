# Villanelle for the End of an Era
::: poem
The era ends, but not the borrowed light.  
We stack its chairs and sweep the glitter clean.  
Tomorrow enters dressed as yesterday's delight.

The slogans fade by morning into white.  
The loud belief grows modest, almost green.  
The era ends, but not the borrowed light.

We call it loss because we liked the height,  
the way the banners fluttered in between.  
Tomorrow enters dressed as yesterday's delight.

What falls apart may only fit more right  
in smaller rooms, in hands no one has seen.  
The era ends, but not the borrowed light.

So ring no bells except the ones that might  
summon the seeds beneath the old machine.  
Tomorrow enters dressed as yesterday's delight.

At closing time the dark is oddly bright.  
The stage goes bare. The grass prepares its scene.  
The era ends, but not the borrowed light.  
Tomorrow enters dressed as yesterday's delight.
:::
