# Autobiography of a Burned Toast

The toast had been bread with prospects.

There had been talk of butter, perhaps jam, even honey on ceremonial occasions. Instead it was lowered into the machine and introduced to heat beyond its qualifications. When it emerged, blackened at the edges and morally transformed, the household considered its death complete.

This underestimated it.

Crushed into crumbs, it thickened soup. Swept into the garden, it fed ants with Protestant appetites. Later, in a dream, it returned as the smell of a kitchen from childhood, which is more than many aristocrats achieve.
