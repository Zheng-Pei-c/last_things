# The Funeral Home for Old Software

The funeral home occupied the third floor above a dentist and specialized in software no longer supported by its creators or understood by anyone's nephew.

Clients brought in floppy disks as if carrying urns. They came with databases from dead companies, love letters trapped in extinct word processors, family recipes saved in formats abandoned by progress. The undertaker, Ms. Lin, wore black cardigans and spoke fluent compatibility.

"Nothing is ever truly lost," she would say, which was not always true but was often enough to keep people breathing while she worked.

Sometimes she succeeded in extracting the files. Sometimes all that remained was the shape of a desire to keep them. In both cases she charged fairly.
