# A Country After Its Flag

The flag came down before the country knew what to call itself.

This was awkward but not fatal. Flags are often given too much credit for coherence.

For a while people spoke in provisional language. Shops kept both signs. Grandparents used old stamps and new curses. Schoolchildren drew maps with dotted borders and optimistic crayons. Then, slowly, the country discovered it had survived the death of one symbol by becoming more than symbol could manage.
