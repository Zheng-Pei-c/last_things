# The Tailor of Second Skins

In a side street behind the cathedral worked a tailor who specialized in second skins.

Widows brought coats that belonged to the dead. Actors brought costumes from roles they had outgrown. Adolescents arrived with school uniforms carrying all the wrong prophecies. The tailor unpicked seams with priestly calm.

"Nothing fits after survival," he said. "That is not your fault."

He lined one jacket with map fabric, another with old prayer cloth, another with the striped silk of a dress too joyful for mourning but too beloved to abandon. Customers left looking less transformed than correctly continued.
