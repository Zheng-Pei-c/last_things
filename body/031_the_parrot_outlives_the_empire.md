# The Parrot Outlives the Empire

The empire collapsed in stages: first the coins, then the flags, then the conviction that maps should obey men in uniforms.

The palace was emptied, catalogued, litigated, and eventually opened on weekends. Only the parrot remained unchanged.

He had spent forty years listening to ministers discuss borders in one room and servants discuss soup in another. By the time tourists arrived, he possessed a multilingual summary of political decline and excellent insults in four dead court dialects.

When the final royal portrait was removed for restoration, the parrot said, very clearly, "Again?"

Everyone applauded because the joke was finally simple enough for history.
